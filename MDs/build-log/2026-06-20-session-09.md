# Build Log · Session 09 · 2026-06-20

> **Goal of this session:** Add **first-run onboarding** so the app feels
> personal from the very first launch — and build the small **settings store**
> it needs.

## What we built

### A settings store (new groundwork)
A simple **key-value store** for app settings/profile — the first thing many
features will lean on (targets, preferences, flags).
- New database table `Settings` (just `key` → `value`).
- **Migration v7 → v8**.
- `SettingsRepository` with `getAll()` / `setValue()`.
- A `SettingsController` (loads settings on startup, exposes them, writes the
  onboarding choices).

### Onboarding flow
A 4-step first-run flow (`OnboardingScreen`), shown only until completed:

1. **Welcome** — a friendly intro to what ME does.
2. **Pick your pillars** — choose which of the five areas to focus on.
3. **Daily action budget** — 3 / 4 / 5 suggestions per day.
4. **Reminder intensity** — low / medium / high (stored for when notifications
   arrive).

On **Get started**, it:
- saves your choices to settings,
- **focuses the app on your chosen pillars** (enables those pillars' habits,
  disables the rest, via the new `setHabitsActiveForPillars`),
- marks onboarding complete — and the app slides into the main screen.

### The "root gate"
`main.dart` now shows a tiny gate that waits for settings to load, then routes:
- onboarding **not** complete → `OnboardingScreen`
- complete → the normal `HomeShell` (the 5 tabs)

### Your daily budget actually does something
The number you pick in onboarding is now **wired into the suggestion engine** —
it controls how many actions Today surfaces. (Previously hard-coded to 5.)

## How it behaves

1. Fresh install → **Welcome** screen.
2. Swipe/Continue through pillars → budget → intensity.
3. **Get started** → lands on Today, showing at most your chosen number of
   actions, drawn from the pillars you picked.

## Note for your existing install

Because your current app predates the settings table, it has no "onboarded" flag
yet — so on the next launch you'll see onboarding **once**. Just pick all five
pillars (the default) and continue, and everything carries on as before with your
existing data intact.

## A new concept worth knowing: app "routing"

Until now the app always opened to the same screen. Now it **decides** what to
show first based on state (have you onboarded?). That decision-point — the
`_RootGate` — is a simple form of **routing**: choosing screens based on
conditions rather than always showing a fixed one.

# Build Log · Session 08 · 2026-06-20

> **Goal of this session:** Close the loop — feed your **reflected energy** into
> the suggestion engine so the app adapts to how you've been feeling. This is the
> moment "Personal OS" starts to behave intelligently.

## What we built

The Today suggestion engine now takes your **most recent reflected energy** into
account when choosing which actions to surface:

| Your energy | What Today does |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **Low** 🪫 | Favours **light** habits; pushes **deep** work down the list |
| **Medium** 🔋 | Balanced (unchanged behaviour) |
| **High** ⚡ | Surfaces the **deep**, demanding tasks |

So after an evening reflection where you mark energy **low**, the next time Today
refreshes it leans toward gentle wins (a short walk, a glass of water) instead of
a 25-minute deep-work block.

## How it works

- The engine already scored each habit on pillar-weakness, streaks, recency and
  difficulty. We made the **difficulty part energy-aware**: the nudge a habit
  gets now depends on *both* its difficulty *and* your energy level.
  - Low energy: light **+12**, medium +3, deep **−8**
  - Medium energy: light +6, medium +3, deep 0 (as before)
  - High energy: light +2, medium +4, deep **+8**
- The **Today controller** reads your latest reflection's energy and passes it
  in. Because it *watches* the reflections data, saving a new reflection makes
  Today re-rank itself automatically.
- If you haven't reflected yet, it defaults to **medium** (same as before) — so
  nothing breaks on a fresh install.

## The change in one picture

```
Evening: you reflect → energy = Low
                         │
                         ▼
Today controller reads latest energy ──► GenerateTodaySuggestions(energy: low)
                                              │
                                              ▼
                 light habits float up · deep work sinks
```

## How we checked it works ✅

| Check | Result |
|-------|--------|
| `flutter analyze` | **No issues** |
| `flutter test` | **16 / 16 passed** |
| `flutter build apk` | **Built successfully** |

Two new tests prove the behaviour directly: with two same-pillar habits (one
light, one deep), **low energy ranks the light one first** and **high energy
ranks the deep one first**. Because the engine is a pure function, we can assert
this without a database or UI.

## Why this matters

This is the payoff of everything before it: reflections (session 07) are no
longer just a diary — they **change what the app recommends**. The five pillars,
the Today screen, and the reflection loop now form one adaptive system, exactly
as the spec envisioned (§6.7.3).

## Note / nuance

We use the **single most recent** reflection's energy. A nice future refinement:
average the last 2–3 days, or weight today's reflection differently from older
ones. Easy to evolve since it's all in one pure function.
